## Fail-Safe and Fail-Secure

Before wiring, it is critical to understand how your lock behaves during a power loss.

<div class="note"><ul><li>Choose <b>Fail-safe</b> for safety-critical areas to ensure that people can exit during power failure.</li><li>Choose <b>Fail-secure</b> for high-security areas to keep the door locked during power loss.</li></ul></div>

### Fail-Safe (Safety First)

- **Status**: The door **unlocks** when power is lost; and remains **locked** when powered.
- **Scenarios:** Emergency exits, fire escape routes, office glass doors, etc.

### Fail-Secure (Security First)

- **Status**: The door remains **locked** during a power outage; and **unlocks** when powered.
- **Scenarios**: Perimeter gates, server room, apartment entrances, etc.
